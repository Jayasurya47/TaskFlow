A local cricket tournament is set to take place in Hyderabad, bringing together talented teams from across the region for an exciting competition. The tournament will feature **10 teams**, each vying for the prestigious title and a grand prize of **₹5 lakhs** for the winning team, while the runners-up will receive **₹2 lakhs**. The matches will be held at the **Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium**, known for hosting major cricket events. Adding to the excitement, **VVS Laxman**, former Indian cricketer and a legend of the game, will be the chief guest, inspiring young players with his presence. The tournament aims to promote local cricketing talent, provide a platform for emerging players, and engage passionate cricket fans in Hyderabad with thrilling matches and sportsmanship. The event will feature league-stage matches followed by knockout rounds, ensuring a competitive and action-packed series. In addition to the main prizes, there will be awards for **Best Batsman, Best Bowler, and Man of the Tournament**, encouraging individual excellence. With a vibrant atmosphere, enthusiastic spectators, and high-level competition, this tournament promises to be a grand celebration of cricket in Hyderabad.

About Chief Guest

VVS Laxman, the chief guest of the tournament, is a legendary former Indian cricketer known for his elegant batting and match-winning performances. Born in Hyderabad, he was one of India's most dependable middle-order batsmen, especially in Test cricket. Laxman is best remembered for his iconic 281-run knock against Australia at Eden Gardens in 2001, which is considered one of the greatest Test innings of all time. His ability to play under pressure and guide India to historic victories earned him immense respect in the cricketing world. After retiring from international cricket, Laxman has contributed to the game as a mentor, commentator, and coach, currently serving as the Director of Cricket at the National Cricket Academy (NCA). His presence at the tournament will be an inspiration for young cricketers, encouraging them to work hard and pursue their dreams in the sport.
